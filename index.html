Encryption, is the method in which plaintext or any other type of data is converted a readable form to an encoded version that can only be decoded by another entity if they have access to a decryption key. Encryption is widely used on the internet to protect user information being sent between a browser and a server. How encryption works: Unencrypted data, often referred to as plaintext, is encrypted using an encryption algorithm and an encryption key. This process generates ciphertext that can only be viewed in its original form of decrypted with the correct key. In order to crack a 256-bit encryption it would take 6.4 quadrillion years.
